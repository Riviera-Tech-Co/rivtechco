Free & low-cost help is also available

If a paid visit isn't the right fit right now, these organizations offer free or low-cost tech support for seniors:

  • Central Coast Commission for Senior Citizens (Area Agency on Aging for Santa Barbara County) — (805) 925-9554 — free tech help programs and referrals for qualifying seniors. centralcoastseniors.org
  • Cyber-Seniors — 1-844-217-3057 — free one-on-one tech help and webinars, nationwide. cyberseniors.org
  • FCC Lifeline Program — a monthly discount on phone or internet service for qualifying low-income households. fcc.gov/lifeline

Joshua Trimmell, Founder

Joshua founded Riviera Tech Co. in 2024 after two decades of hands-on technology experience — from managing enterprise accounts at companies like Yardi Systems to running network operations as an MSP engineer and overseeing HIPAA-compliant systems as an IT Director. He holds a B.S. in Telecommunications & Management.

His connection to senior tech support goes back further than his career, though. Joshua grew up around his mom's beauty shop, surrounded by her regulars — mostly women in their 70s, 80s, and 90s who welcomed a curious kid into their conversations. People have always told him he's an "old soul," and he's been comfortable around older folks ever since. These days that shows up in a practical way, too: helping his own parents navigate the tech that increasingly runs daily life.

That's the philosophy behind Riviera Tech Co.'s senior support — patient, unhurried, and judgment-free. If it takes explaining something three different ways to get you comfortable with your phone or laptop, that's just part of the job.
